# Editorial Policy

1. Prefer official ICH, FDA, EMA, European Commission, regulation, guidance, and procedural sources.
2. Separate source status from website editorial-review status.
3. Classify statements as required, conditional, recommended, potentially impacted, best practice, or undetermined.
4. Summarize and cite; do not reproduce large portions of source documents.
5. Record the official URL, relevant location, source status, and last verification date.
6. Label unreviewed synthesis `Editorial draft — source verification required`.
7. A human-review label requires a review record. Author and reviewer roles must be recorded even when one person performs both in separate sessions.
8. Product-specific guidance, correspondence, commitments, waivers, and scientific context may change expectations.
9. Correct material errors promptly and retain the change history.
